General Help/Support

Where can I get academic and study skills support?

The University offers a range of support services for students to assist with their studies from their first day right through to graduation. For information on the full range of services available, please visit our support page. You can make an appointment with a Student Progress Advisor to find out more about the free support the university offers and how they can help you create a plan for your success.

Academic Learning Support runs workshops that students can register for free of charge. Workshops range from Academic Writing Skills to Mathematics, and are suitable for enabling, undergraduate, postgraduate and research higher degree students. Students also have the opportunity to have individual consultations with Learning Advisors and mid-year workshops are also run before the start of semester to help students further develop their academic writing skills. Further information can be found at Academic Learning Support.

Students can also access the Study Skills support page.

Academic Learning Support offers free workshops, individual consultations, e-consultations, weekly review sessions and online resources through Blackboard to help you with the development of your academic skills.

The Online Resources will help you to build your library and research skills and is fully accessible online.  

The University offers a number of support services including learning support, health and wellbeing, financial support, program advice and much more. For information on the full range of services available, please visit Support.

The University also offers a number of study support programs delivered by high achieving and trained students. PASS (Peer Assisted Study Sessions) operates for many first year courses. These sessions provide a space where students can work through concepts, ask questions and discuss lecture details each week, working with a PASS Leader who facilitates these sessions. PASS timetables and details can be found on our website.

PASS Leaders also operate as Peer Writing Mentors, these mentors are located in the University's libraries and provide one on one peer support and advice about getting the most from your academic writing.

There is a wide range of student support services available to help students transition to university and get the most out of their study.
Our support services can provide you with assistance in the following areas:
  • Health and Wellbeing
  • Meeting the Costs of Study
  • Program Advice
  • Student Life on Campus
  • Tools for Success
  • Other Topics (student living, chaplaincy, counselling, disability support, careers service etc)
You may also like to check out the Peer Mentor program. Peer Mentors are experienced students who understand the challenges and rewards of being a new student at the University and are here to help you settle into study at the University of Newcastle.

Student Progress Advisors, part of the Progression and Success Team, are also available to provide support to students. They can work with you to help you make the most of your time at University and to support your successful journey through your studies. Student Advisors are campus and program specific and are available for face to face, online and phone appointments. They can also be contacted via email at studentadvice@newcastle.edu.au.

Our Academic Learning Support team also offers advice and support on essay writing, preparing for exams, English and Maths skills and they host a range of workshops and courses.

You can also look out for the ""Ask Me"" pop up stands that are often placed around campus. The staff on these stands can help you with any questions, and if they can't answer them for you they will make sure you know where to go to get the correct support. Look out for the blue ""Ask Me"" signs.

Visit Support for further information on the services available.

Register with AccessAbility and you will be provided with an Reasonable Adjustment Plan. This plan can then be provided to your course co-ordinators.

See AccessAbility for more information.

Adverse circumstances

Adverse Circumstances are issues beyond your control that affect your ability to complete assessments, sit your exam or have a negative impact on your performance.

Acceptable reasons for an Adverse Circumstances application are:

  • health grounds
  • compassionate grounds
  • hardship
  • trauma
  • unavoidable commitments

Adverse Circumstances do not include:

  • misreading the Course Outline or examination timetable.
  • usual work commitments.
  • travel plans.
  • being unaware of the assumed knowledge requirements for your course

Note: You must tell the University about your adverse circumstances in advance of your assessment being due and prior to your exam.

If the issue arises during the exam, you must advise the Exam Supervisor of your intention to lodge an adverse circumstances application before leaving the exam.

After you have accepted your offer, to activate your account log back in to myHub six hours later using your UAC number as your username and your date of birth as your password (in the format dd/mm).

This process can be done from your home computer, a publicly accessible computer (please ensure that you log out when you have finished) or an online Account Activation Terminal at various locations within the University. These do not require a login.

Click on Student Login Details and your student number and program code will be displayed. Write this information down, as you will need it again.

Click on the Account Activation hyperlink to continue to the next step and set your password. Once you activate your student account you will gain access to important University online systems.

Note: If you have studied at the University of Newcastle before you will still be required to use your Application Number (not your Student Number) and Date of Birth to accept your offer (ddmm).

Detailed instructions on how to enrol are available online or you can refer to the how to enrol video. (you may need to try different browsers e.g Internet Explorer or Safari)

Overseas/Incomer (someone new to Newcastle)

Overseas Student Health Cover (OSHC) is insurance that helps cover the cost of medical care for international students while they are living in Australia, which in some instances, applies to pharmaceuticals and ambulance services. It is a mandatory requirement for all student visa holders in Australia. International students must maintain OSHC for the duration of their student visa, as do any family members, such as partners and children.

The University of Newcastle, is working with Allianz Global Assistance to assist students to streamline this process. International students can signup with the help of the three steps provided below:

Step 1: Register for online services - Visit allianzassistancehealth.com.au and create an online account
Step 2: Download the My OSHC Assistant app
Step 3: Download the Doctors on Demand app

If you need help with any of these processes and want to know more about the benefits you can get from this insurance. you can visit the locations mentioned below.

  • Callaghan Campus, Hunter Building, UON Global (Monday to Wednesday 9am to 5pm)
  • Newcastle City Precinct NUspace on Thursday 10am to 4pm.

You will need to log in to myUON and select the 'Attendance Check-In' tile. You will have 5 minutes before the start of class and 45 minutes before the end of class to check in.

Please note:
  • Location services must be enabled on your device when checking-in
  • If you arrive after the check-in window has closed or you have forgotten please speak to your teaching staff who, in certain circumstances, will be able to check you in
  • You can check-in using a laptop, desktop or tablet, or you may also check-in on another student’s device provided you use your own myUON login
  • You have to be in the classroom to check-in
  • Another student cannot check-in on your behalf
  • Only your attendance and location at the time you check-in will be recorded
  • You can only attend the class session that you are allocated to in My Timetable. You will not be able to check-in to any class activities that you are not allocated in the timetabling system. If your need swap classes on an ongoing basis you will need to update your class allocation

  • Last day to add a course: this is the last day you may add a course through myHub. The last day to add a course is the second Friday of the semester. If you need to enrol after this date, you should complete an Application for Late Enrolment, which must be approved by the Course Coordinator.
  • Census Date: this is the last day you may withdraw from a course without being financially liable (having to pay). This is also the last day that eligible students may submit their request for HECS-HELP to defer their tuition fees. This is also the last day to submit an SA-HELP eCAF to defer your Student Services and Amenities Fee (SSAF). You can access the HECS-HELP and SA-HELP request in myHub. You can view your other payment options at Fee Payment.
  • Last day to withdraw from a course: this is the last day you can withdraw from a course without academic penalty. This is the last Friday of the semester. Your enrolment in the course will not appear on your Official Academic Transcript, however you will be financially liable for the course (you may apply for a remission or refund in extenuating circumstances).
  • Examinations: There is a formal exam period which runs for three weeks commencing at the conclusion of each semester. You will be informed of your examination timetable around Week 9 via email sent to your NUmail.
  • Fully Graded Date: This is the day that your semester results are released. You will not be able to access your grades if you have any holds on your record, including the Academic Integrity Module, library fines, or miscellaneous fees, visit What can I do to ensure I can view my grades when results are released on Fully Graded Date?
