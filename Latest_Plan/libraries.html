Auchmuty Library◢

Shortland, Callaghan

The Auchmuty Library is located on the Western side of the campus near the Shortland Union Building. The Library service zone, Learning Lounge and the Law collection are located on the main level (level 3). The Information Common (AIC), Course Readings collection, Cafe and Cultural Collections are located on level 2. The Learning Lounge is open 24 hours and provides group study spaces, collaborative computer desks, WiFi and a Survival Station with food preparation area. Library staff are not available to provide assistance outside library hours. The area is designed for collaborative study. Bring your own device (BYOD) is recommended as individual computer spaces are limited. Laptops are available for loan from the Auchmuty Information Commons. Swipe card access is required for entry outside Library opening hours.

Auchmuty Information Common (AIC)

The AIC is open 24 hours and provides access to scholarly information and resources in a relaxed, individual or group workspace. This area also includes a Survival Station with food preparation area. Wireless and laptop facilities, access to PCs and Macs, scanners, print/copy and laptop borrowing are available. Rovers assist with basic IT support. The Information Service Desk and a café are also located in the AIC. Staffing times vary. Swipe card access is required for entry outside Library opening hours.

Auchmuty Library Family Spaces

The Auchmuty Carers and Kids Room is located on level 3 in room L325. This room includes bookable PCs, computer desks, and a collection of children's books, toys, puzzles and play tables. Children must be supervised at all times while in the Library. The Auchmuty Parent Room is located on level 3 in room L324. This room provides infant care facilities for feeding and nappy changing.

Huxley Library◢

Hunter, Callaghan

The Huxley Library is located on Levels A and C of the Hunter Building on the Callaghan Campus. It specialises in supporting the areas of Education, Nursing, Medical Radiation Science, Fine Art and Photography. Access to the Huxley Library on the weekends is via the main entrance to the Hunter Building. The Curriculum Resources & Media Collection (CRMC) contains a wide range of educational resources designed to support the research and teaching needs of trainee teachers. Group study tables are also provided in the CRMC. Huxley also has an extensive AV collection, quiet study carrels, computer labs, a postgraduate room and group study rooms.

Huxley Information Common

The Huxley Information Common is located in the Huxley Library. It has 9 group study booths, PC express terminals, laptop benches, PCs in group pods, lounges and a central print/copy area. A self-check in and out machine and an EFTPOS autoloader is located nearby.

Newcastle City Campus Library◢

NUspace, Newcastle

The Newcastle City campus Library in UON's NUspace building houses a dynamic collection of high demand and core items to support the needs of staff and students located at the Newcastle City campus. The Library has group study rooms, collaborative areas, computer work stations, lounges and quiet study spaces. 24 hour common areas are available on the ground and first floors of the NUspace building. Swipe card access is required for after hours entry. Use your UON ID card to release and pay for printing at printer/photocopiers throughout NUspace.

Library Services and 24 hour spaces in NUspace

During Library Service opening hours find general library assistance on the ground floor of the NUspace building as well as general and specialist support in the Library on Level one. 24 hour common areas are available on the ground and first floors of the NUspace building. Swipe card access is required for after hours entry.
