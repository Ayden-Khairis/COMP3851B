Accomodation

Need a place to stay? learn about the various ways you can find accommodation on and off campus and which one would suit you.

Accomodation

On-Campus

Living on campus is a great choice for students as it allows them close proximity to their studies. he university offers many ways to support students on-campus and ensure a safe and positive environment Student Living (UoN). The picture above shows the different residences, there are 8 of them on campus. You are likely to find the right environment for your needs here, depending on your budget, lifestyle, values and studies. Below are the color code meanings for different areas explained:

  • ⬤ Lifestyle areas are for residents who wish to live in a quieter living environment with minimal alcohol usage while still enjoying Student Living activities.
  • ⬤ Areas for P, H and RHD for residents who are studying postgraduate, honours or research degrees and are seeking a quieter environment to maximise study time.
  • ⬤ Gender areas for residents that wish to live with those of the same gender only. If you live in a Gender area, guests of the opposite gender will not be allowed to stay overnight
  • ⬤ General areas for residents who do not wish to select any specific living environment.

Temporary

Many international students and some domestic students may require short stay accommodation while looking for something more permanent or other reasons. You can find out options here Short Stay Accomodation (UoN).

Homestay

Homestay is a great way to learn about Australian culture, practice your English and make new friends. The University of Newcastle’s Homestay program, established since 1998, offers a safe, affordable and family-oriented accommodation that enables you to live and interact with local families. Find out what is included in your Homestay and what you can contribute to your family here: Homestay (UoN).

Commute

Getting to uni and going to other places? learn about public and university transport services, as well as information for drivers.

Commute

Public Transport

The campus is well-serviced by bus and train routes. The Transport for NSW website here can help plan your trip.

If you’re a full time, on-campus student you may be entitled to a travel concession. Visit ASKUON if you'd like to check your eligibility. Applying for the Concession Opal Card is a two-step, online process. Don’t forget you'll need to apply for your Opal Concession card each year. Concession eligibility (UoN).

Active Travel

Active travel focuses on physical activity (walking, cycling, scooters, skateboards and the use of public transport) as your transport mode, which offers a range of great health, financial and environmental benefits. Active travel options are some of the most efficient and effective ways to incorporate regular exercise into your daily life. And with showers, lockers and secure bicycle storage located in the Bike Hubs, 9 you can be active and still feel fresh for class. The Hubs are free and can be accessed with your student card, with two located at Newcastle (Callaghan) and one at Newcastle City. To support a healthier University community we have put together a range of Active Travel Guides to consider when looking at your travel options. Active Travelling (UoN).

Drive and Park

With the campus spread out over 140 hectares of natural bushland, it’s a good idea to arrive early and give yourself plenty of time to walk from your parking space to your destination. You can buy a full year or six-month parking permit from the University’s online shop and collect it from Student Central. Otherwise buy a daily parking ticket from the machines around campus. The machines do not give change but do take paywave, Apple and Samsung Pay. Find designated free parking and free motorcycle parking areas on the campus maps. Disabled parking bays are for RMS Mobility holders only. You must park in designated “General” parking areas only. Parking in staff or reserved parking or on landscaped areas will attract a hefty fine. There is free parking for motorcycles in the designated bays. Drive and Park (UoN).

Park and Ride

A free shuttle bus for staff and students runs from Callaghan to Newcastle City campus. The shuttle bus operates 5 days a week from 7am – 10:15pm, usually every 30 mins. You can use this service to travel between classes all you need is your student ID card. To see the timetable and pick-up points Shuttle Bus (UoN).

Ridesharing

Ridesharing is a great way to save money, enjoy company on your drive and reduce your carbon footprint. The UoN is partnered with Liftango an exclusive ridesharing app with exclusive free car parks. You can download the Liftango App through the Google Play store on Android devices or The Apple Store on iOS devices. Ridesharing (UoN).

Personal Support

Need personal support? the university offers many ways to support students in their personal lives and mental health.

Personal Support

Learning Support

The Learning Support team is there to help guide students through their studies should they ever feel like they do not know how to approach an assignment. From help with essay writing to mathematics questions and even help with the English language, the Learning Support team is there to help you out.

Peer assisted Study Sessions (PASS) and Online PASS (OPASS)

The PASS program is designed to give students the opportunity to gather and discuss course related content with their piers, this includes comparing notes and the review of weekly materials. There is a PASS leader for each session, this leader has achieved high marks in their course and runs the sessions. For more information on PASS or OPASS check out the PASS and OPASS university page.

Indigenous Learning Support

The Wollotuke Institute offers learning support for Indigenous students, check out this link: Indigenous Student Support .

Health Services

The university provides medical centres staffed by doctors and nurses at both the Newcastle (Callaghan) and Central Coast (Ourimbah) campuses. All consultations are strictly confidential for more information follow the link: Health Services.

Mental Health Support

The university takes mental health very seriously and it should always be a priority for you, therefore the univeristy offers students Counselling that they can access face to face on campus, online or by telephone.
