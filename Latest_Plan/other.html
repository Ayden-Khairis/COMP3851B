Assistance

If you have any struggles or require extra assistance, the services below can provide huge amounts of help.

Make sure you click the orange headings if you would like to learn more about these services.


Student Central

Student Central focuses on student support, experience, support, and services. The best student experience comes when you feel supported academically, professionlly, and socially. Student Central is the first point of call if you have any questions about literally anything to do with University and if they can't answer your question, they will lead you in the right direction to ensure your question gets answered.


IT Service Desk

IT Support can be contacted by reporting an issue online at serviceUON via the IT Support tile on myUON, or via phone from Monday-Friday 8am-6pm on 02 4921 7000. The problems IT Support can help you with include problems with student email, student accounts, computers, passwords, wireless networks, and downloading software.


Prepare for Success

If you're feeling overwhelmed about your courses, want to learn how to become organised, or think you might need help creating a plan of action for this semester, you can create a Prepare for Success plan! This plan can provide you with the help you need to succeed in your first semester. If you're unsure of what to put in it or want to seek some additional help with your plan, you can speak to a Student Progress Advisor and they will give you the extra help you require.


PASS

PASS stands for Peer Assisted Study Sessions. These free, online sessions are run by students who previously received high grades in your course. One hour of PASS study is the equivalent of three hours of solo study. You can ask questions, compare notes, and discuss your coursework with people who may offer different perspectives than your own. Majority of first year courses have PASS, so feel free to use this resource.


Health Services

The University's Health Service is a fully accredited general practice staffed by doctors, nurses, and dentists who understand student's medical problems. If you ever have any health issues and seek the need of one of these professionals, be sure to contact them at any time. The medical centre is open to all Newcastle University students.


Mental Health

University semesters can be stressful, especially when at times you may feel like you have no time to complete your assignments or study for your exams. You may also be going through some hardships in life and need someone to talk to. The University's counselling service is free and confidential for all students who need help with personal or study related issues. Telephone and online counselling is also available if required.


Indigenous Support

If you are an Indigenous Australian, you will have heaps of support to help you succeed. Newcastle University can help you with financial assistance, specialised tutoring, and much more. The Wollotuka Institute has Indigenous staff to help you with all your needs.
